Full Help & Walkthrough

Quick Start (3 Steps)

  1. Sign up for API keys (all free): Etherscan V2, MegaNode, Helius. Use the "Get Key ↗" buttons next to each field — they open the signup pages in new tabs.
  2. Paste your wallet addresses into the textarea, one per line, prefixed with eth:, bsc:, or sol:.
  3. Click Fetch & Calculate. Wait 1–15 minutes depending on transaction volume.

Why Three Different APIs

Each blockchain has its own explorer. A single universal crypto API doesn't exist on free tiers. Good news: every key is site-wide, not per-wallet. One Etherscan key reads every public Ethereum address.

  • Etherscan V2 → Ethereum mainnet history
  • MegaNode (BSCTrace) → BNB Smart Chain history. Required because BscScan was deprecated December 18, 2025 and BSC is no longer in Etherscan's free tier.
  • Helius → Solana history. Auto-parses Jupiter/Raydium swaps into clean data — essential for memecoin traders.
  • CoinGecko → historical USD prices. No key needed.

Finding Your Wallet Addresses

⚠ This is the single most important input to get right. The tool needs every wallet you've ever used — not just your current main one. Missing even one old archived wallet causes two cascading failures: self-transfers between your wallets get marked as taxable sales, AND spending from the "unknown" wallet later produces "missing cost basis" errors in FIFO. Take the extra 5 minutes to dig through old accounts.

MetaMask (ETH + BSC)

  1. Open MetaMask. Click the account dropdown at the top.
  2. For each account listed — current, old, archived, hardware-linked — click the account name. It copies the 0x... address (42 chars).
  3. MetaMask uses the same address on both Ethereum and BSC. If you've used both networks, list each address twice: once as eth:0x... and once as bsc:0x....
  4. Don't skip an old account just because "there's nothing in it now" — you may have sent the funds out, and if the destination wallet isn't also listed, FIFO breaks.

Phantom (Solana)

  1. Open Phantom. Click the wallet name at the top to see the account selector.
  2. For each Solana account in the list, click it and copy the address (base58, 32–44 chars, no 0x).
  3. Add each as sol:ADDRESS on its own line.
  4. Hardware wallets connected to Phantom show up in the same selector — include those too.

Other wallets to check

  • Old Trust Wallet, Coinbase Wallet, Atomic Wallet, Exodus installations
  • Solflare, Backpack (Solana)
  • Any hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor) — get the addresses from the companion app or from Etherscan/Solscan using transaction history
  • Any wallet generated during a specific protocol interaction (e.g., a one-time wallet for a launchpad) that you later abandoned but may have transferred from/to
Iterative discovery technique: Run the tool once with your main wallet. In the transactions table, filter by status "Review" and look at the counterparty addresses (fromAddr / toAddr) on transfers you recognize as "I sent this to myself". Those are wallets you forgot to list. Add them and re-run. Repeat until the "missing cost basis" errors stop appearing. Each iteration is cached so re-runs are fast.

Running the Tool

  1. Fill in all four API keys and every wallet address, then click Fetch & Calculate.
  2. Watch the live run log for progress. The run proceeds through these phases:
    1. Transaction fetch — Etherscan (ETH), MegaNode (BSC), Helius (Solana). Fast: usually <30 seconds.
    2. Internal transfer tagging — instant, uses your wallet list to identify non-taxable self-transfers.
    3. Token name resolution — Jupiter verified list bulk fetch, then per-mint search for unknowns. ~30 seconds for a typical wallet.
    4. CryptoCompare pricing — historical USD prices for major coins (SOL, ETH, BTC, USDC, etc.). ~300ms per unique (coin, date) pair. Takes from a few seconds to a couple of minutes on first run depending on how many unique pairs exist.
    5. Swap-pair price derivation — for memecoins and other tokens CryptoCompare doesn't index, the tool derives a USD value from the swap's priced counterpart. Instant, no network calls. This handles pump.fun tokens, Jupiter/Raydium memecoin trades, and anything else where one side of the swap is a known coin. See the FAQ for details on how this works and why it's IRS-correct.
    6. FIFO engine — instant, processes the priced data into gain/loss records.
  3. All API results cache in your browser. Re-runs are near-instant unless you click Clear Cache.

Understanding the Results

Summary cards show short-term and long-term realized gains for the selected tax year only. "Needs Review" counts ALL transactions across history that couldn't be processed automatically.

Status badges:

  • OK (green) — successfully priced and in FIFO calculations
  • Review (yellow) — missing price, unknown token, or basis mismatch. Fix manually before filing.
  • Internal (gray) — transfer between your own wallets. Not taxable.

Common "Needs Review" reasons (in order of frequency):

  • Missing cost basis for X SOL/ETH/BNB — no matching acquisition lot — FIFO saw a disposal of this asset but couldn't find a matching earlier acquisition. Usually caused by a missing wallet address in your config. The acquisition happened in a wallet you didn't list, so the tool never saw the incoming lot. Add the missing wallet and re-run. If the acquisition was truly off-chain (Coinbase, KuCoin, MEXC, tip.cc, or another centralized service), you'll need to reconstruct basis manually or treat it as a zero-basis acquisition at FMV on receipt date.
  • Unknown token — not in known-asset list — tool saw a token transfer but couldn't price it via CryptoCompare AND couldn't derive a price from a swap counterpart. Usually a one-sided receipt: airdrop, gift, or transfer from a centralized service (tip.cc, an exchange). For these, you'll need to research the price manually at DexScreener, Birdeye, or Solscan for that timestamp, or treat as zero-basis at receipt.
  • DEX swap — unknown token, manual review required — same root cause as above, but the row is a swap specifically with no priceable counterpart. Rare: occurs when both sides of a swap are unknown memecoins (token-for-token swap of two unlisted assets).
  • CryptoCompare price lookup failed — rare; usually a transient API issue. Re-run to retry.

What you will NOT see in the review list anymore: Pump.fun memecoins, Jupiter/Raydium swap tokens, and other unknown tokens that were acquired or disposed via a swap with a priceable counterpart (SOL, USDC, USDT, etc.). Those now get their USD value derived automatically from the swap counterpart and flow into FIFO normally. Look for the note Price derived from swap counterpart in the transactions table.

Fixing Needs-Review Items

  1. Click Needs-Review CSV to download just the flagged rows.
  2. Open in Excel/Sheets.
  3. Research each price at DexScreener, Birdeye, or CoinGecko.
  4. For exchange-sourced tokens, pull the Coinbase CSV and match by date+amount.
  5. For unrecoverable records (old KuCoin/MEXC), document a reasonable-method reconstruction with your CPA.
  6. The planned database version will persist manual overrides so you fix each one once.

Troubleshooting

"NOTOK" or "Invalid API Key" from Etherscan: Double-check the full key was copied. Fresh Etherscan keys take 1–2 minutes to activate server-side after creation.

MegaNode returns empty for BSC: Verify BSC Mainnet is enabled for your NodeReal key in the dashboard sidebar (BSC / BNB Chain must be toggled on).

Helius returns empty for Solana: Use the wallet address from Phantom, not a sub-account or token account address. Solana wallet addresses are base58, 32–44 chars, no 0x prefix.

CryptoCompare Test Key fails: The Test Key diagnostic shows the exact HTTP response. If it's HTTP 401 or 403, the key was copied wrong — delete the field and re-copy from the CryptoCompare dashboard. Don't try to type the key manually; it's easy to mistake characters like 0/O or l/1.

Everything flagged "Missing cost basis": You're missing wallet addresses. Look at the review rows — each has a fromAddr and toAddr column. Any address there that you recognize as yourself needs to go into the Wallet Addresses field. Re-run after adding. This is almost always the fix; truly off-chain basis (from exchange deposits or defunct services) is rarer than people expect.

Memecoins still flagged as "Unknown token" after a run: Swap-pair derivation handles memecoins acquired or disposed via DEX swaps automatically. If a memecoin still appears in Needs Review, it's typically because it was a one-sided receipt (airdrop, gift, tip, transfer from an exchange) with no corresponding outflow in the same transaction — so there's no priceable counterpart to derive a value from. For these, research the price manually at DexScreener/Birdeye/Solscan or treat as zero-basis on receipt.

FAQ

Q: How does the tool price pump.fun / Jupiter memecoin trades if no aggregator tracks them?
A: Swap-pair price derivation. For every DEX swap, the tool looks at both sides of the transaction. If one side is a token CryptoCompare knows about (SOL, USDC, USDT, etc.) and the other side is an unknown memecoin, the tool assigns the memecoin's USD value equal to the priced side's total USD value. Per-unit price is derived as USD value divided by quantity. Example: you swap 0.055 SOL (CryptoCompare says $10.89) for 1,193,950 "6fig" tokens. The tool assigns $10.89 as the cost basis for that 6fig acquisition, making the implied per-6fig price $0.00000912.

Q: Is swap-pair derivation IRS-correct?
A: Yes. For crypto-to-crypto trades, IRS guidance (and longstanding practice for capital assets generally) holds that fair market value is the value of what was given up. The tool's derived value is literally the value of what you gave up (or received) in the same transaction, which is the cleanest possible evidence of fair market value — arguably better than a market-average aggregator quote from a different timestamp. Transaction fees paid in the same transaction are included in the derived value, which is also IRS-correct (fees are part of cost basis for capital assets).

Q: Which cases does swap-pair derivation NOT handle?
A: Three cases get left in Needs Review:

  1. Orphan receipts — tokens that arrived in your wallet without a corresponding outflow (airdrops, gifts, tips, withdrawals from centralized services like Coinbase or tip.cc). No counterpart exists, so no derivation possible. For these, research the market price manually or treat as zero-basis on receipt.
  2. Orphan disposals — tokens that left your wallet without a corresponding inflow (sent as a gift or tip to someone else). No counterpart exists.
  3. Both-sides-unknown swaps — rare cases where you swapped one unlisted memecoin directly for another unlisted memecoin. Neither side is priceable, so no derivation works.

Q: Can I use this for TY2019–2024?
A: Yes. Change the tax year field. Tool always fetches complete history so FIFO lots carry across years. Wallet-by-wallet tracking became mandatory Jan 1, 2025 per IRS Rev. Proc. 2024-28.

Q: What if FIFO can't find acquisition basis for a disposal?
A: This happens when tokens entered your wallet from a source the tool didn't scan (an exchange withdrawal, tip.cc, another wallet you forgot to list, or a misclassified swap). The tool flags these as orphan disposals and surfaces them in the Manual Cost Basis Overrides panel below the realized gains table, sorted by approximate proceeds (impact). For each one you can: ignore it (skip from 8949), enter a manual basis you researched from your records, or treat it as zero-basis (100% gain). Big-dollar rows are worth investigating; dust is fine to leave alone. Overrides persist in your browser across runs.

Q: What if a row is unpriced — neither CryptoCompare nor swap-pair derivation could attach a USD value?
A: The tool surfaces these in the Unpriced Transactions panel below the cost basis overrides panel. Two flavors: orphan receipts (incoming tokens like airdrops or tip.cc deposits — set the cost basis you paid) and unpriced disposals (memecoin sells where neither side could be priced — set the proceeds you received). For each row, choose to ignore it, enter a USD value, or treat as zero. After clicking Save & Re-Apply, the receipt becomes a normal acquisition lot in FIFO and the disposal flows through to the 8949 with the correct gain/loss. In TurboTax exports these are tagged [MANUAL] or [ZERO] as Buy or Sale rows.
